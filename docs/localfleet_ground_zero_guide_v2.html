Core Concepts — What You're Actually Building

No jargon. Just what things are and why they matter.

What Is This Project?

You're building a demo that works like this: you talk into a microphone (or type a sentence), and simulated boats AND a drone on a map start moving in coordinated patterns. That's it. That's the whole thing.

The magic is in what happens between your words and things moving:

Hockey Camp Analogy

Think of it like running your hockey camp. You shout "Line 1, run the power play drill! Goalie coach, watch from the stands!" Your assistant coaches hear that, figure out which kids go where AND tell the goalie coach to observe from above. Your project does the same thing — but with boats and a drone instead of kids, and an AI "assistant coach" that interprets your commands and routes them to the right domain.

  1. Your voice/text gets converted to written words (by Whisper, a speech-to-text AI)
  2. Written words get interpreted by a large language model running on YOUR machine into structured instructions — "vessel Alpha goes here, drone Eagle sweeps there, in this pattern"
  3. Structured instructions get routed by DOMAIN — surface commands go to CORALL (boat simulator), air commands go to the drone model
  4. Asset positions get streamed in real-time to a web dashboard so you see boats AND drones on the same map
NEW IN v2.0

The original plan was surface vessels only. After researching HavocAI's actual direction (they just acquired a drone company and a ground vehicle company), this version adds a simulated drone that operates alongside your boats, commanded through the same natural language interface. It also adds a GPS-denied mode — a toggle that degrades position accuracy, mirroring HavocAI's most impressive demo (air-sea coordination without GPS in Portugal, December 2025).

What Is CORALL?

CORALL is an open-source research project from Queen's University Belfast. Think of it as a boat simulator that already knows maritime traffic rules. Researchers already built the hard parts: how boats move through water, how they detect collision risk, and how they follow the international rules of the road (COLREGs). It's written in Python and free to use (MIT license).

You are NOT building a boat simulator from scratch. You're taking CORALL's simulator and adding three things on top: (1) an AI that understands English commands for BOTH boats and drones, (2) a simple drone model that flies alongside the boats, and (3) a pretty dashboard to watch it all happen.

What Is the Drone Model?

It's the simplest possible simulation of a flying thing. It has a position (x, y coordinates on the map), an altitude (how high it is), a heading (which direction it's pointing), and a speed. You give it waypoints and it flies toward them. It can orbit a point in circles, sweep across an area in straight lines, or track one of the boats from above.

This is NOT realistic drone physics. Real drone simulation involves wind, battery, rotor dynamics — PhD-level stuff. Yours is more like a dot on a map that moves smoothly and happens to have an altitude readout. That's enough. The point isn't simulating drone aerodynamics — it's showing that your C2 system can command assets across different domains through one interface.

What Is GPS-Denied Mode?

In the real world, military systems often operate in environments where GPS has been jammed or spoofed by an adversary. HavocAI demonstrated their system working without GPS in Portugal in December 2025 — it was a huge deal.

Your GPS-denied mode is a toggle on the dashboard. When you flip it, three things happen: (1) position reports get "fuzzy" — random noise is added so positions jump around slightly, (2) update rate drops from 4 times per second to 1, and (3) the dashboard shows visual indicators (pulsing circles around assets) showing uncertainty. It's cosmetic but it signals awareness of contested environments — something HavocAI cares deeply about.

What Is Ollama?

Ollama runs AI language models on your own computer. Normally, ChatGPT runs on OpenAI's servers. With Ollama, the AI runs entirely on your Mac Studio. Nothing leaves your machine. In defense, this matters enormously because military data cannot go to the cloud (ITAR regulations).

What Is a Pydantic Schema?

Shipping Label Analogy

Imagine every package had a label with exactly these fields: sender, receiver, weight, dimensions, contents. If any field is missing or the wrong type, the post office REJECTS it immediately.

A Pydantic schema is exactly that — the exact shape of data flowing between parts of your program. If the AI outputs a command missing a field, your code catches it instantly. In v2.0, these schemas now include a domain field on every command and state — so the system always knows whether it's talking about a boat or a drone.

What Is a WebSocket?

A normal website is like texting: request, response, done. A WebSocket is like a phone call — once connected, both sides talk continuously. Your dashboard needs real-time updates as boats and drones move. WebSocket keeps the "call" open and streams positions constantly.

What Is React?

React builds interactive web interfaces from small reusable "components" — a map component, an asset card component, a command input component — snapped together like LEGO. When data changes (boat moves, drone changes altitude), React updates just the affected parts of the screen.

What Is Leaflet?

Leaflet puts interactive maps on web pages using free OpenStreetMap tiles. You add markers (your boats and drones) and move them as data streams in. With one CSS trick, it looks like a military command screen. In v2.0, you use different icons for surface vessels versus drones so they're visually distinct.

Multi-Domain Explained — Why Air + Sea Matters

Why we added a drone. Why HavocAI cares. Why this makes you stand out.

What Does "Multi-Domain" Mean?

"Domains" in military terms are the environments where you operate: sea (surface and undersea), air (drones and aircraft), land (ground vehicles), space, and cyber. When the military says "multi-domain operations," they mean coordinating forces ACROSS these environments simultaneously.

HavocAI started in the sea domain. In March 2026, they acquired two companies to expand into air (Mavrik — military drones) and land (Teleo — autonomous ground vehicles). Their whole pitch is now: one operator commands assets across all domains through one interface.

Hockey Camp Analogy

Imagine you're running a tournament — not just your ice hockey camp, but simultaneously a street hockey clinic in the parking lot and a goalie training session in the gym. Three different "domains" of hockey. Each has its own rules, its own equipment, its own playing surface. But you're the coach coordinating all three from your office, watching camera feeds, giving instructions through radios. That's multi-domain C2.

How Your Drone Fits In (Technically)

Your system has two types of assets:

Surface Assets (alpha, bravo, charlie)

Use CORALL's vessel dynamics — realistic physics, COLREGS compliance, CPA/TCPA risk assessment. These are the complex, research-grade simulation pieces that come from the open-source project.

Air Asset (eagle-1)

Uses your simple drone model — position, altitude, heading, speed, waypoint following. Can orbit a point, sweep an area, or track a surface vessel from above. Simple but effective on the dashboard.

Both types live in the same Fleet Manager, respond to the same natural language commands, and appear on the same dashboard map. The LLM figures out which commands go to which domain automatically. When you say "Alpha patrol the harbor, Eagle recon from altitude" — the system routes "Alpha patrol" to a CORALL instance and "Eagle recon" to the drone model.

GPS-Denied Mode — What It Looks Like

When you toggle GPS-Denied on the dashboard:

  • Asset positions start "jittering" — a few meters of random noise added each update
  • Update rate drops from 4Hz to 1Hz — positions update less frequently
  • Dashboard shows pulsing circles around each asset indicating uncertainty radius
  • Assets continue operating autonomously — they don't stop just because GPS is degraded

This is cosmetic — you're not simulating actual inertial navigation systems. But the visual effect on the dashboard communicates an important concept: these systems need to keep working even when the environment degrades. That's exactly what HavocAI demonstrated in Portugal.

Mental Models — How to Think About This Project

The frameworks that will keep you sane. Updated for multi-domain.

The Tournament Director Model

How Your Multi-Domain Project Works

Think of your project like directing a hockey tournament with multiple rinks:

  • Tournament Director (you) — gives orders in English ("Rink A, run the 5-on-5 drill! Upstairs gallery, film from above!")
  • Walkie-talkie (Whisper) — converts your voice to text
  • Head coordinator (LLM via Ollama) — reads the order, figures out which rink and which camera crew to direct, writes specific instructions for each
  • Instruction sheet (Pydantic schema) — standardized format with a "domain" checkbox: ICE RINK or GALLERY. Every instruction must specify which domain.
  • Ice rink coaches (CORALL vessel instances) — handle the on-ice stuff: skating physics, collision avoidance, play execution
  • Gallery camera crew (drone model) — handle the aerial view: position, altitude, sweep patterns
  • Fleet Manager (tournament scheduler) — routes ice instructions to ice coaches, gallery instructions to camera crew
  • Scoreboard feed (WebSocket) — streams everything live
  • Big screen (React dashboard) — shows ice AND gallery on the same display

The instruction sheet with the domain checkbox is what holds the whole tournament together.

The "Working Beats Perfect" Rule

The Rule

A working demo of 3 boats and a drone moving on a map is worth more than a perfectly architectured system that never runs. At every step: "Does it work? Can I record it?" If yes, record it. If it works, it ships.

The "Integration, Not Invention" Mindset

You're NOT inventing new AI. NOT writing boat physics from scratch. NOT simulating real aerodynamics. You're assembling proven pieces into a new combination: CORALL (proven boat sim) + Ollama (proven local LLM) + a simple drone model + React dashboard + GPS noise function. The value is in the assembly and the multi-domain coordination — not in any one piece.
